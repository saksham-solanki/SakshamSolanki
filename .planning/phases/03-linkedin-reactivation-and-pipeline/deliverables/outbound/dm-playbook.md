# Outbound DM Playbook

**Signal-Based, Engagement-First Outbound for $40K+ Decision-Makers**

---

## 1. The Signal-Based Outbound Philosophy

Spray-and-pray is dead. 79% of decision-makers ignore cold DMs in 2026. Merge-field "personalization" ({first_name}, {company_name}) is detected instantly and deleted. The volume game -- sending 100+ connection requests per day and hoping for replies -- produces reply rates under 5%.

What works: engagement-first, signal-based outbound.

**The principle:** Engage with a prospect's content for 5-7 days before connecting. When you send the connection request, your name is already familiar. When you send the first DM, you have context they can feel.

**Signal-based > merge-field personalization.** One authentic, specific sentence referencing something the prospect actually said, did, or announced outperforms three paragraphs of templated "personalization." A signal is a real event: a funding round, a new hire, a LinkedIn post about a challenge, a portfolio acquisition. Merge fields are data points. Signals are human moments.

**The math:**
- Cold connection requests: ~30% acceptance rate, 5-9% DM reply rate
- Signal-based, engagement-first: 60-70% acceptance rate, 15-25% DM reply rate
- Content-to-pipeline (inbound from your posts): 14.6% conversion vs 1.7% for cold

This playbook is designed around signals. Every connection request, every DM references a specific trigger event. Nothing is copy-pasted. Nothing is mass-sent.

Trigger events are the fuel for signal-based outbound. Monitor these daily using Sales Navigator alerts, LinkedIn notifications, and manual checking.

## 3. The 7-Day Warm Engagement Sequence

Before sending a connection request, warm the prospect for 5-7 days. This turns a cold request into a warm one. The prospect recognizes your name when the request arrives.

**Day 1:** Like their most recent post. No comment yet. Just show up in their notifications.

**Day 3:** Leave a thoughtful comment on their next post. Add genuine value. NOT "great post" or "love this." The comment must demonstrate that you read and understood their content.

**Day 5:** Like and comment again, or engage in a thread they are active in. If they do not post frequently, comment on a shared connection's post where they are in the thread.

**Day 7:** Send connection request with a personalized note referencing your shared context (see Section 4).

### Comment Approaches That Add Value Without Selling

**Approach 1: Add a data point they did not mention.**
If they post about operational efficiency, comment with a specific stat or finding from your experience. Example: "This matches what I have seen deploying automation for mid-market companies -- the 120-hour-per-week manual processes are always hiding in the same three departments: support, finance, and operations. Support is usually the fastest win because the data is already structured."

**Approach 2: Ask a question that extends the conversation.**
Not a softball question. A question that shows you understand the domain. Example: "Curious whether you are seeing the same pattern with portfolio companies -- the ones that try AI as a 'pilot' almost always stall at deployment. Have you found a way to skip the pilot stage and go straight to production?"

**Approach 3: Share a contrarian or nuanced perspective.**
Disagree respectfully or add nuance. This stands out because most comments are agreement. Example: "I would push back slightly on the timeline -- 12-18 months for an internal AI team to ship is the average, but the real issue is not speed. It is that internal teams build what they know, not what the business needs. External teams bring pattern recognition from dozens of deployments."

## 4. Connection Request Examples (Per ICP)

## 5. Post-Acceptance 3-Step DM Sequence

After a prospect accepts your connection request, begin the value-first DM sequence. This is NOT a pitch sequence. This is a diagnostic conversation that naturally leads to an offer.

### PE Firm DM Sequence

**DM 1 (Day 1 after acceptance): Thank + Ask About Portfolio AI Challenges**

The goal is to start a conversation, not deliver a monologue. Reference the trigger event from your connection request.

*Example Sequence A (New Acquisition trigger):*

> Thanks for connecting, [Name]. I noticed [Company]'s recent acquisition of [Portfolio Co] -- interesting space.
>
> Curious question: across your portfolio, are you seeing AI initiatives that stall after the pilot stage? That is the pattern we keep running into -- the proof of concept works, but production deployment never happens.
>
> Would love to hear how you are thinking about AI execution at the portfolio level.

*Example Sequence B (Operating Partner post trigger):*

> Appreciate the connection, [Name]. Your post about measuring AI impact across the portfolio stuck with me.
>
> We have been deploying production AI systems for mid-market companies -- the kind where we define the EBITDA target before we build and measure against it after deployment. 14 deployments, zero failures.
>
> What is the biggest operational bottleneck you are seeing across your current portfolio companies?

**DM 2 (Day 3): Share Relevant Case Study as Insight**

Only send if DM 1 got a response OR if the prospect viewed your profile after DM 1. If no response and no profile view, wait until Day 7.

*Example A (they mentioned support/ops challenge):*

> Thought this might be relevant to what you mentioned about [specific challenge].
>
> We recently helped a similar-sized company automate their support operations -- 68% of tickets now resolve automatically, resolution time dropped 73%. The operating partner used the deployment as the model for three other portfolio companies.
>
> Happy to share more details on how the rollout worked if it is useful.

*Example B (they mentioned AI strategy execution):*

> Following up on the AI strategy execution point -- this might resonate.
>
> We worked with a PE-backed company that had an "AI strategy" sitting in a board deck for 8 months. Went from strategy document to deployed system in 45 days. The operating partner reported a quantified P&L impact at the next investment committee meeting.
>
> Specific enough to be useful, or is the challenge different in your case?

**DM 3 (Day 7): Offer Free AI Readiness Assessment**

Only send if the conversation has been warm (they responded to DM 1 or DM 2). If cold, do NOT send -- move the prospect to the "Parked" list.

*Example:*

> Based on our conversation, it sounds like [specific portfolio company or portfolio-wide challenge] could benefit from a structured look at where AI would actually move the needle.
>
> Happy to run a free AI readiness assessment for [portfolio company name] -- takes about 30 minutes, no strings. We map the highest-impact automation opportunities and quantify the projected savings.
>
> If that is useful, DM me "audit" and I will send the details.

### Funded Startup DM Sequence

**DM 1 (Day 1 after acceptance): Thank + Ask About AI Feature Roadmap**

*Example Sequence A (Fresh Funding trigger):*

> Thanks for connecting, [Name]. Congrats again on the Series B -- exciting milestone.
>
> Curious: with the fresh capital, is AI on the product roadmap? We have been helping startups at your stage ship production AI features in 60 days -- without pulling engineers off core product work.
>
> What is the biggest thing your engineering team is trying to ship right now?

*Example Sequence B (AI Job Posting trigger):*

> Appreciate the connection, [Name]. I noticed [Company] is hiring for AI -- that search can take a while.
>
> Several CTOs at your stage have bridged the gap by bringing in an external team to deploy the first AI systems while they build the internal team. We shipped 50,000 active users on an AI platform in 60 days for one client.
>
> Is the AI hire for a specific feature, or more of a general capability build?

**DM 2 (Day 3): Share Case Study About Shipping AI Fast**

*Example A (they mentioned needing AI features):*

> Thought this might be relevant to what you mentioned about [specific feature/goal].
>
> We recently helped a startup ship an AI-powered [relevant feature] -- from kickoff to 50,000 active users in 60 days. The CTO kept their core team on the main product while we handled the entire AI workstream.
>
> The biggest surprise was the timeline. They expected 6 months. We deployed in 8 weeks.

*Example B (they mentioned engineering capacity):*

> On the engineering capacity point -- this is the exact pattern we see with Series A-B companies.
>
> One client was burning $150K/month with a 6-person engineering team already at capacity. We came in as an embedded AI team, deployed a system that tripled their qualified meetings at $150/month operating cost. Their CTO said it freed up two engineers to focus on core product instead.
>
> Is your engineering bandwidth the bottleneck, or is it more about knowing what to build?

**DM 3 (Day 7): Offer Free AI Readiness Assessment**

*Example:*

> Based on what you shared about [specific challenge or goal], it sounds like there might be a fast path to shipping [specific AI feature or solving specific problem].
>
> Happy to run a free 30-minute AI readiness assessment for [Company] -- we would scope whether AI would actually accelerate [specific feature or metric] and what a 60-day deployment would look like.
>
> If useful, DM me "audit" and I will set it up. No charge, no strings.

## 6. Content-to-Pipeline Conversion

This runs PARALLEL to cold outbound and typically converts at 14.6% vs 1.7% for cold. Content engagement is the warmest signal you can get.

### Daily Content-to-Pipeline Workflow

**Step 1: Monitor post engagement (5 min)**
After your daily post has been live for 2-4 hours, check who engaged:
- Who liked the post?
- Who commented?
- Who shared?
- Who viewed your profile in the last 24 hours?

**Step 2: Filter for ICP matches (5 min)**
For each engager, quick-check:
- Job title: Does it match PE operating partner, portfolio CEO, startup CEO/CTO?
- Company size: 11-200 employees?
- Company type: PE firm, PE portfolio company, Series A-C startup?

If yes, they enter the pipeline.

**Step 3: Route based on connection status**

| Status | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Not connected, matches ICP | Add to 7-Day Warm Engagement Sequence (Section 3) |
| Already connected, matches ICP | Send a DM referencing the specific content they engaged with |
| Connected, already in DM sequence | Note the engagement in your tracking sheet -- it is a buying signal |

**Step 4: DM for connected ICP engagers**

When a connected ICP prospect likes or comments on your post, send a DM within 24 hours:

> Appreciate you engaging with the [topic] post, [Name]. That [specific point from the post] is something we have been seeing a lot with [PE portfolio companies / startups at your stage].
>
> Is [related challenge] something you are running into at [Company]?

This is not selling. This is converting a content interaction into a conversation.

## 7. Objection and Non-Response Handling

### No Response to DM 1

**Do:** Wait. Do not follow up for at least 3 days. Continue engaging with their content (likes, comments) to stay visible.

**On Day 3:** Send DM 2 (the case study share). Frame it as a standalone value add, not a "just following up" message.

**If no response to DM 2:** Wait until Day 7. Send DM 3 (the audit offer) only if they have viewed your profile or engaged with your content since DM 1. If not, move to "Parked" list.

**Do NOT:** Send "bumping this" or "just checking in" messages. Every message must carry value.

### They Respond But Are Not Interested

**Response: "Not a priority right now"**
> Totally makes sense. I will keep sharing relevant insights in my posts -- if the timing changes, the offer stands. Enjoy connecting regardless.

**Response: "We handle this internally"**
> Good to hear -- you are further ahead than most. If you ever need a second opinion on a specific deployment or want to benchmark against what we see externally, happy to chat informally.

**Response: "Too expensive" (if pricing comes up)**
> Understood. For context, most of our clients find the engagement pays for itself within the first quarter -- the Audit alone typically identifies 5-10x its cost in operational savings. But timing is everything. Let me know if the math changes.

**In all cases:** Stay connected. Keep engaging with their content. Keep them in your feed. Timing changes. Budgets shift. The conversation you started today may close in 90 days.

### They Respond With Interest

**Response: "Tell me more" or "That sounds interesting"**

Move immediately to booking a discovery call. Do not over-explain in DM.

> Great to hear. The best way to dig into this is a quick 30-minute call where I can ask a few questions about [Company]'s specific situation and map the opportunities.
>
> Here is my calendar: [Calendly link]
>
> Or if you prefer, just share a couple of times that work this week and I will send an invite.

### LinkedIn Account Safety -- Flagging Risk

- If your connection request acceptance rate drops below 40%, reduce daily volume by 50% immediately.
- If you receive a "restricted" warning, stop all outbound for 48 hours, then resume at 50% volume.
- Keep pending (unaccepted) connection requests under 500 at all times. Withdraw old pending requests weekly.
- Never send identical messages to 30+ people in one hour. Every message must be individually written.

## Usage Notes

This playbook is designed for manual execution. Automation tools (Expandi, Dripify) are explicitly deferred until the messaging is validated through manual outbound. Automate only after you have proven:
1. Connection request acceptance rate is consistently above 40%
2. DM reply rate is consistently above 15%
3. You are booking at least 2 discovery calls per week from outbound

Until then, every message is hand-written. The quality of manual outreach is the competitive advantage.
