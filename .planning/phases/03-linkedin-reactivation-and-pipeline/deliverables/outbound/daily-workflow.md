# Daily Outbound Workflow

**Step-by-step daily routine for generating qualified conversations with $40K+ decision-makers.**

---

## 1. Time-Blocked Daily Schedule

Total time: 90-120 minutes per day. Execute in this order every day.

### Block 1: Morning Engagement (15-20 min) -- Before Posting

**Purpose:** Build visibility with target prospects and thought leaders. Algorithm rewards engagement activity -- your own posts get more distribution when you are actively engaging.

1. Open LinkedIn. Go to your feed.
2. Find 15-20 posts from target ICP accounts and AI thought leaders.
3. Like each post.
4. Leave 3-5 thoughtful comments (see DM Playbook Section 3 for comment approaches).
5. Prioritize commenting on posts from:
   - Prospects currently in your 7-Day Warm pipeline
   - ICP decision-makers you want to connect with
   - AI thought leaders whose audience overlaps with your ICP
6. Do this BEFORE posting your own content. Non-negotiable.

### Block 2: Post Content (5 min)

**Purpose:** Maintain daily posting cadence.

1. Publish the day's post from your weekly batch (drafted on Sunday).
2. If using a scheduler (Taplio/Buffer), verify the scheduled post went live.
3. If posting manually, copy from your drafts and publish.
4. No edits after posting (algorithm resets engagement when you edit within the first hour).

### Block 3: Prospect Identification (15-20 min)

**Purpose:** Fill the top of the outbound pipeline with new ICP prospects.

1. Open Sales Navigator.
2. Check "Alerts" tab for new matches, job changes, and funding events.
3. Review your saved searches for new leads (10 per day).
4. For each new lead:
   - Verify they match ICP criteria (title, company size, industry)
   - Check their recent LinkedIn activity (do they post? are they active?)
   - Note the trigger event (funding, new role, relevant post, hiring)
   - Add to Google Sheets tracking with all details
5. Add these 10 new prospects to the 7-Day Warm Engagement pipeline.

### Block 4: Warm Engagement (15-20 min)

**Purpose:** Execute engagement actions for prospects in the 7-day warm pipeline.

1. Open your tracking sheet. Filter to prospects in "Engaging" status.
2. For each prospect at their scheduled engagement day:
   - Day 1 prospects: Like their most recent post
   - Day 3 prospects: Leave a thoughtful comment
   - Day 5 prospects: Like and comment again
   - Day 7 prospects: Ready for connection request (move to Block 5)
3. Update the engagement status column in your tracking sheet.

### Block 5: Connection Requests (10-15 min)

**Purpose:** Send personalized connection requests to warm prospects.

1. Filter tracking sheet for prospects who completed the 7-day warm sequence.
2. For each one, write a personalized connection request note:
   - Reference the specific trigger event you noted
   - Keep under 300 characters
   - Do NOT pitch or mention services
   - See DM Playbook Section 4 for examples
3. Send the request.
4. Update tracking sheet: status = "Request Sent," date = today.
5. Maximum daily volume: see graduated ramp-up schedule (Section 2).

### Block 6: DM Sequences (15-20 min)

**Purpose:** Move connected prospects through the 3-step DM sequence.

1. Check for new connection acceptances (last 24 hours).
2. For new acceptances: Send DM 1 (thank + ask about challenges). Update tracking sheet.
3. Check tracking sheet for prospects due for DM 2 (Day 3 after acceptance).
4. Check tracking sheet for prospects due for DM 3 (Day 7 after acceptance).
5. Write each DM individually -- reference the specific trigger event and conversation history.
6. See DM Playbook Section 5 for sequence structure and examples.

### Block 7: Response Management (10-15 min)

**Purpose:** Capitalize on engagement and conversations.

1. Reply to all comments on your own post (target: within 15 minutes of each comment for 90% algorithmic boost -- check periodically throughout the day if possible).
2. Respond to DM replies from prospects. If they express interest, move immediately to discovery call booking.
3. Check "Who viewed your profile" in the last 24 hours. Filter for ICP matches.
4. For ICP profile viewers not yet in your pipeline: add to tracking sheet and begin 7-day warm.
5. For ICP profile viewers already in pipeline: note the profile view as a buying signal in tracking sheet.

### Block 8: Evening Review (5 min)

**Purpose:** Maintain pipeline accuracy and plan tomorrow's actions.

1. Update tracking sheet:
   - Move prospects through pipeline stages
   - Note all responses received today
   - Flag any prospects who booked or indicated interest in a call
2. Review tomorrow's engagement targets (who needs Day 3 comment? who is ready for connection request?).
3. Note any trigger events discovered today that need action tomorrow.

## 2. Graduated Ramp-Up Schedule

**This is non-negotiable for a dormant account.** LinkedIn limits are reputation-based. A dormant account that suddenly sends 25 connection requests per day will get flagged. The ramp-up protects the account.

### Week 1: Engagement Only (Days 1-3 of posting)

| Activity | Daily Volume |
|----------|-------------|
| Likes | 10-15 |
| Comments on others' posts | 3-5 |
| Post own content | 1 per day |
| Connection requests | ZERO |
| DMs | ZERO (warm reactivation only -- see network-reactivation.md) |

**Goal:** Reactivate the algorithm. Signal to LinkedIn that this is a real, active account. Build engagement momentum.

### Week 1-2: Soft Outbound (Days 4-7)

| Activity | Daily Volume |
|----------|-------------|
| Likes | 15-20 |
| Comments | 5-8 |
| Post own content | 1 per day |
| Connection requests | 10 per day |
| DMs | Warm reactivation DMs only (10-15/day) |
| New prospects entering warm pipeline | 10 per day |

**Goal:** Begin building the outbound pipeline. 10 connection requests per day with personalized notes. Start the 7-day warm engagement for the first batch of prospects.

### Week 2-3: Full DM Sequences (Days 8-14)

| Activity | Daily Volume |
|----------|-------------|
| Likes | 15-20 |
| Comments | 5-8 |
| Post own content | 1 per day |
| Connection requests | 15 per day |
| DM 1 (new acceptances) | As they come in (target 5-8/day) |
| DM 2 (Day 3 follow-ups) | As scheduled |
| DM 3 (Day 7 offers) | As scheduled |
| New prospects entering warm pipeline | 10-15 per day |

**Goal:** Full pipeline operational. Connection requests, DM sequences, and warm engagement all running simultaneously.

### Week 3+: Target Volume (Days 15+)

| Activity | Daily Volume |
|----------|-------------|
| Likes | 20+ |
| Comments | 5-10 |
| Post own content | 1 per day |
| Connection requests | 20-25 per day |
| Full DM sequence | Pipeline-dependent |
| New prospects entering warm pipeline | 15 per day |

**Goal:** Steady-state outbound. 20-25 connection requests per day, full DM pipeline, content-to-pipeline conversion running in parallel.

### Safety Rules

- **Pending requests:** Keep under 500 at all times. Withdraw unaccepted requests older than 2 weeks every Sunday.
- **Acceptance rate:** If it drops below 40%, reduce connection request volume by 50% immediately. Review your connection request notes -- are they personalized enough?
- **Restriction warnings:** If LinkedIn sends ANY restriction warning, stop all outbound for 48 hours. Resume at 50% volume. Investigate what triggered it.
- **Message pattern detection:** Never send identical or near-identical messages to more than 5 people in an hour. Every message must be individually written.
- **Daily spread:** Spread connection requests and DMs throughout the day. Do not send all 20 requests in a 15-minute window.

## 3. Pipeline Tracking System (Google Sheets)

Use Google Sheets for pipeline tracking during Phase 3. CRM integration (HubSpot) is Phase 4.

| DM 2 Date | When DM 2 was sent | 2026-03-20 |
| DM 2 Response | Yes/No + summary | Yes - interested in case study |
| DM 3 Date | When DM 3 was sent | 2026-03-24 |

### Engagement Status Values

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Watching | Identified, not yet engaging |
| Engaging - Day X | In 7-day warm sequence, currently on Day X |
| Request Sent | Connection request sent, awaiting acceptance |
| Connected - DM X | Accepted, in DM sequence at step X |
| Responded - Warm | Responded positively, conversation active |
| Call Booked | Discovery call scheduled |
| Parked | Not interested now, revisit later |
| Not a Fit | Disqualified |

## 4. Weekly Metrics and Targets

Track these every Sunday during the content batch session.

| Metric | Week 1 Target | Week 2 Target | Week 3+ Target |
|--------|---------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Connection requests sent | 0 (engagement only) | 70 (10/day) | 140-175 (20-25/day) |
| Acceptance rate | N/A | >40% | >40% |
| DMs sent | Warm reactivation only | Begin sequences | Full pipeline |
| DM response rate | N/A | >10% | >15% |
| Profile views from ICP | Track baseline | Increasing | Consistently increasing |
| Post engagement rate | Track baseline | >4% average | >4% average |
| Discovery calls booked | 0 | 1-2 (from warm reactivation) | 2-3 per week |

### The Ultimate Metric

**Discovery calls booked per week.** Everything else is a leading indicator. If calls are not materializing by Week 3, revisit:
1. Is the connection request acceptance rate above 40%? If not, messages are not personalized enough.
2. Is the DM response rate above 15%? If not, case studies are not resonating or timing is off.
3. Are ICP prospects engaging with your content? If not, content topics may not be matching their interests.

## 5. ICP Split Strategy

Maintain a 50/50 split between PE firms and funded startups in your daily outbound.

| Day Part | PE Prospects | Startup Prospects |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Prospect identification | 5 new PE leads | 5 new startup leads |
| Warm engagement | Split across both ICPs | Split across both ICPs |
| Connection requests | Half to PE, half to startups | Half to PE, half to startups |
| DM sequences | ICP-matched messaging | ICP-matched messaging |

This 50/50 split is a starting point. After 2-3 weeks of data, adjust based on which ICP has higher acceptance rates, higher DM response rates, and more discovery calls booked. Double down on what works.
