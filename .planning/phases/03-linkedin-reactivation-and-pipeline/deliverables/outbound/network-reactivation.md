# Warm Network Reactivation Plan

**Audit existing connections, reactivate ICP matches, and convert them to discovery calls -- starting Day 1.**

---

## Why Warm First

Existing connections who respond have dramatically higher conversion rates than cold outbound because trust already exists. You do not need to build credibility from scratch -- they already accepted your connection at some point. The conversation starts from familiarity, not cold introduction.

Warm reactivation runs from Day 1 -- even before cold outbound begins (which waits until Day 4). These are the fastest path to your first discovery calls.

---

## Step 1: Audit Existing Connections (Days 1-2)

### Connection Search Method

LinkedIn does not export connections with filtering, so use the platform's search within your connections.

1. Go to "My Network" then "Connections"
2. Use the search bar within connections to find ICP matches
3. Search each of the following terms separately:

**PE-related searches:**
- "Operating Partner"
- "Portfolio"
- "Private Equity"
- "Managing Director"
- "Value Creation"
- "VP Operations" + filter by Financial Services

**Startup-related searches:**
- "CEO" + filter by company size (use LinkedIn search filters)
- "CTO"
- "Co-founder"
- "VP Engineering"
- "Head of Product"
- "Series A" or "Series B" (in their headline or about)

**General high-value searches:**
- "Founder"
- "Chief Technology"
- "Chief Operating"
- Names of specific companies you know are PE-backed or funded startups

### Building the Shortlist

Target: 50-100 warm connections matching ICP criteria.

For each match, record in your Google Sheets "Warm Reactivation" tab:

| Field | What to Note |
|-------|-------------|
| Name | Full name |
| Company | Current company |
| Title | Current title |
| ICP Type | PE / Startup / Other |
| Last Interaction | When you last messaged or engaged (check message history) |
| Shared History | Any context: mutual connections, past project discussions, met at event, engaged with their content |
| Relevance | Why they would care about AI systems (based on their company/role) |
| Priority | High (strong shared history), Medium (some context), Low (connected but no history) |

### Categorization

Sort your shortlist into three buckets:

**Bucket 1 -- High Priority (reactivate first):**
- You have had real conversations before
- They engaged with your content in the past
- You share mutual connections who could reinforce credibility
- Their company clearly matches ICP (PE firm, funded startup)
- Target: 15-25 connections

**Bucket 2 -- Medium Priority:**
- You are connected but interactions were minimal
- Their role/company clearly matches ICP
- No strong shared history, but the connection exists
- Target: 25-40 connections

**Bucket 3 -- Low Priority (reactivate after Buckets 1 and 2):**
- Connected, minimal or no interaction history
- ICP match is less clear but possible
- Target: 15-35 connections

## Step 2: Warm Reactivation DM Sequence

### General Principles

- This is genuine reconnection, NOT outreach disguised as catching up.
- Reference something real: shared history, their recent activity, or what you have been building.
- Keep it casual. These people already know you -- talk like it.
- Do NOT pitch in the reactivation DM. The sequence moves naturally from reconnection to conversation to offer.

### The 3-Step Reactivation Sequence

**Reactivation DM 1: Casual Reconnection**

Timing: Day 1 of reactivation (start with Bucket 1)
Goal: Restart the relationship. Get a response.

Framework:
- Acknowledge the gap in communication
- Share what you have been building (briefly, with proof)
- Ask about what they are working on (genuine curiosity)

**Reactivation DM 2: Share Relevant Insight (Day 3-5 after DM 1, IF they respond)**

Goal: Deliver value specific to their role/industry.

Framework:
- Reference something from their response
- Share a relevant case study or insight matched to their situation
- Frame as "thought you would find this interesting," not as a pitch

**Reactivation DM 3: Offer Free Assessment (Day 7 after DM 1, IF conversation is warm)**

Goal: Convert the warm conversation into a discovery call.

Framework:
- Reference the conversation thread
- Offer the free AI readiness assessment for their company
- Position as no-commitment, quick diagnostic
- Clear CTA: "DM me 'audit' if useful"

## Step 3: Example Reactivation DM Sequences

**These are EXAMPLES showing how to make it feel like genuine reconnection, not outreach. Adapt to your actual relationship with each person.**

### Example Sequence 1: PE Operating Partner

**DM 1 (Reconnection):**

> Hey [Name], it has been a while. I went heads-down for the past 5 months building AI systems -- shipped 14 production deployments across several industries. The PE world has been particularly interesting -- portfolio companies are sitting on massive operational waste that AI can eliminate in weeks, not months.
>
> How are things going at [Fund Name]? Are your portfolio companies starting to look at AI operationally, or is it still mostly exploratory?

**DM 2 (Day 3, after they respond about portfolio AI challenges):**

> That matches what I am seeing across the board. Most portfolio companies try a pilot, it sits in a demo environment, and nobody deploys it.
>
> We just finished a deployment for a similar-sized company -- automated their support triage and eliminated 68% of tickets. The operating partner used the results to build the business case for rolling it out across three other portfolio companies. Quantified EBITDA impact went straight to the investment committee deck.
>
> If you are seeing that pattern across your portfolio, happy to share more about how the rollout worked.

**DM 3 (Day 7, conversation is warm):**

> Based on what you mentioned about [specific portfolio company or challenge], it sounds like a structured look at where AI could move the P&L would be valuable.
>
> I am offering free AI readiness assessments for PE portfolio companies -- takes 30 minutes, no strings. We map the highest-impact opportunities, quantify projected savings, and give you a clear picture of what is worth pursuing.
>
> If that would be useful for [portfolio company name], DM me "audit" and I will send the details.

### Example Sequence 2: Startup CEO/CTO

**DM 1 (Reconnection):**

> [Name], long time. I disappeared from LinkedIn for a while -- was heads-down shipping AI systems. 14 production deployments in 5 months, including an AI platform that hit 50,000 active users in 60 days.
>
> How is [Company] going? Last I remember you were working on [reference something from their profile or past interaction]. Are you looking at AI features on the product roadmap?

**DM 2 (Day 4, after they respond about product roadmap or scaling challenges):**

> That is exactly the pattern I keep seeing with Series [A/B/C] companies. Engineering team is at capacity on core product, board wants AI features, and hiring takes 6+ months.
>
> We just helped a startup in a similar spot ship an AI-powered feature -- from kickoff to production in 60 days. Their CTO kept the core team on the main product while we handled the entire AI workstream as an embedded team. Biggest win: they saved 60 hours per week of manual content work that was bottlenecking growth.
>
> Is the bottleneck more about engineering capacity or figuring out what to build?

**DM 3 (Day 7, conversation is warm):**

> From what you shared about [specific challenge or feature], it sounds like there is a clear path to shipping [specific AI feature] faster than you might think.
>
> Happy to run a free 30-minute AI readiness assessment for [Company] -- we would scope whether AI could accelerate [specific goal] and what a 60-day deployment would realistically look like.
>
> DM me "audit" if useful. No charge, no pitch.

### Example Sequence 3: General High-Value Connection

**DM 1 (Reconnection):**

>

**DM 2 (Day 3-5, after they respond):**

>
> If that sounds useful, DM me "audit" and I will send the scheduling link. No commitment.

## Step 4: Daily Reactivation Volume and Timing

### Volume

- **Days 1-7:** 10-15 warm reactivation DMs per day (Bucket 1 first, then Bucket 2)
- **Days 8-14:** 5-10 reactivation DMs per day (continuing through Buckets 2 and 3) + cold outbound DMs begin
- **Days 15+:** 3-5 reactivation DMs per day (finishing Bucket 3, plus any new ICP connections who accept from cold outbound)

### Timing Within the Day

Send reactivation DMs during Block 6 (DM Sequences) of the daily workflow. Warm reactivation DMs come BEFORE cold DM sequences because warm prospects are higher probability.

### Tracking

Use the "Warm Reactivation" tab in your Google Sheets tracker. Same column structure as the main pipeline tracker, plus:

| Additional Column | Description |
|-------------------|-------------|
| Shared History | What is your actual connection to this person |
| Bucket | 1 (high), 2 (medium), 3 (low) |
| Last Interaction Date | When you last communicated |

## Step 5: What Success Looks Like

### Week 1 (reactivation-heavy)

- 50-100 connections audited and categorized
- 10-15 reactivation DMs sent per day (70-105 total)
- Response rate target: 25-35% (these are warm connections)
- First conversations started, first "interested" signals captured
- 1-2 discovery calls booked from warm connections (stretch goal)

### Week 2 (reactivation + cold outbound begins)

- Remaining Bucket 2 and Bucket 3 connections reactivated
- Cold outbound DM sequences begin for newly accepted connections
- Response rate tracking: warm vs cold comparison
- 2-3 discovery calls booked (warm reactivation + early cold pipeline)

### Week 3+ (steady state)

- Warm reactivation list is mostly exhausted
- Pipeline is a mix of warm reactivated and cold outbound connections
- Focus shifts to cold outbound + content-to-pipeline conversion
- 2-3 discovery calls per week target

### Key Insight

Warm reactivation will likely produce your first discovery calls faster than cold outbound. This is by design -- existing connections already trust you. Use these early calls as reps to sharpen your discovery framework (Phase 4) before the higher-volume cold pipeline starts producing calls.
