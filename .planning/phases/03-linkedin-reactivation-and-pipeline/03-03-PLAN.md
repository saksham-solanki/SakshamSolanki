**dm-playbook.md — The complete outbound playbook:**

This is the core outbound system. It must be genuinely personalized, NOT templated (user explicitly rejected generic approaches). Build around the signal-based, engagement-first approach from research.

**Section 1: The Signal-Based Outbound Philosophy**
- Why spray-and-pray is dead (79% of decision-makers ignore cold DMs in 2026)
- The engagement-first principle: engage with prospect content for 5-7 days before connecting
- Signal-based > merge-field personalization. One authentic specific sentence outperforms three paragraphs of {first_name} "personalization"

**Section 2: Trigger Event Checklist (per ICP)**

PE Firms trigger events to monitor:
- New portfolio company acquisition
- Operating partner hire
- Portfolio company posting about "digital transformation" or "operational efficiency"
- Portfolio company layoffs/restructuring (efficiency opportunity)
- Fund raise announcements

Funded Startups trigger events:
- Series A-C funding announcement (within 30-60 days)
- CTO/VP Engineering hire
- Job postings for AI/ML roles (signal: they need AI but building in-house is slow)
- Founder posting about scaling challenges
- Product launch or major feature release

**Section 3: The 7-Day Warm Engagement Sequence (before connection request)**
- Day 1: Like their most recent post
- Day 3: Leave a thoughtful comment (add genuine value, NOT "great post")
- Day 5: Like and comment again, or engage in a thread they're active in
- Day 7: Send connection request with note referencing shared context
- Include 3 example comment approaches that add value without selling

**Section 4: Connection Request Templates (per ICP)**

Create 5 connection request note examples per ICP (PE and Startup) — NOT templates to copy-paste, but EXAMPLES showing how to weave trigger events into personalized notes. Each example:
- References a specific type of trigger event
- Feels like one human reaching out to another
- Does NOT pitch or mention services
- Under 300 characters
- Different tone/angle from the others

Explicitly note: "These are EXAMPLES of the approach, not templates to copy-paste. Each real request must reference the specific prospect's actual trigger event."

**Section 5: Post-Acceptance 3-Step DM Sequence**

PE Firm DM sequence:
- DM 1 (Day 1 after acceptance): Thank + ask about AI challenges across portfolio. Use PE language (EBITDA, margin expansion, operational value creation). Reference a specific post/trigger event.
- DM 2 (Day 3): Share relevant case study matched to their portfolio industry. Frame as insight, not pitch. "We helped a [similar company] achieve [specific result]."
- DM 3 (Day 7): Offer free AI readiness assessment for one portfolio company. Position as no-commitment diagnostic. "Happy to run a quick AI readiness assessment for [portfolio company] — no strings. DM me 'audit' if interested."

Funded Startup DM sequence:
- DM 1 (Day 1): Thank + ask about their AI feature roadmap. Use startup language (ship faster, burn rate, runway). Reference their product/funding.
- DM 2 (Day 3): Share case study about shipping AI features fast (Sprint results). Frame as what's possible.
- DM 3 (Day 7): Offer free AI readiness assessment. Position as speed advantage. "We can scope whether AI would accelerate [specific feature/goal] in a free 30-min call."

Write 2 full example DM sequences per ICP (4 total) showing real-sounding personalized messages. Mark them as examples, not copy-paste scripts.

**Section 6: Content-to-Pipeline Conversion**
- Monitor who engages with your posts (likes, comments, shares, profile views)
- Check if engager matches ICP criteria
- If yes and not connected: add to 7-day warm engagement sequence
- If yes and already connected: send DM with related value
- This runs PARALLEL to cold outbound and typically converts at 14.6% vs 1.7% for cold

**Section 7: Objection and Non-Response Handling**
- What to do when DM 1 gets no response (wait, don't follow up aggressively)
- What to do when they respond but aren't interested (graceful exit, stay connected)
- What to do when they respond with interest (move to discovery call booking — Phase 4)
- LinkedIn flagging risk: if acceptance rate drops below 40%, reduce daily volume

**daily-workflow.md — Step-by-step daily outbound routine:**

Create the complete daily workflow that Saksham follows every day once outbound begins (Day 4+ for soft outbound, Day 8+ for full DM sequences).

**Time-blocked daily schedule (total ~90-120 min/day):**
1. Morning engagement block (15-20 min): Comment on 15-20 target ICP and thought leader posts BEFORE posting own content
2. Post content (5 min): Publish daily post from batch (drafted on Sunday)
3. Prospect identification (15-20 min): Use Sales Navigator saved searches to identify 10 new prospects matching ICP. Check trigger events. Add to tracking sheet.
4. Warm engagement (15-20 min): Execute engagement actions for prospects in the 7-day warm pipeline (likes, comments on their posts)
5. Connection requests (10-15 min): Send connection requests to prospects who completed 7-day warm. Personalized notes referencing trigger events.
6. DM sequences (15-20 min): Send DMs to newly accepted connections (Day 1 DMs), follow-up DMs (Day 3, Day 7) to those in pipeline.
7. Response management (10-15 min): Reply to all comments on own posts, respond to DM replies, check profile views for ICP matches.
8. Evening review (5 min): Update tracking sheet, note tomorrow's engagement targets.

**Graduated ramp-up schedule (CRITICAL for dormant account safety):**
- Days 1-3 of posting: Engagement only (likes, comments). NO connection requests. NO DMs.
- Days 4-7: Add 10 connection requests/day. Begin warm engagement pipeline.
- Days 8-14: Full DM sequences begin. Increase to 15 connection requests/day.
- Days 15+: Reach target volume of 20-25 connection requests/day. Full pipeline operational.
- Safety rules: Keep pending requests under 500. If acceptance rate drops below 40%, reduce volume by 50%. Watch for restriction warnings.

**Pipeline tracking system (Google Sheets):**
- Columns: Prospect Name, Company, ICP Type (PE/Startup), Title, Trigger Event, Engagement Status (Watching/Engaging/Connected/DM Stage), Connection Request Date, DM 1 Date, DM 2 Date, DM 3 Date, Response, Notes, Next Action Date
- Tabs: Active Pipeline, Connected (in DM sequence), Responded (warm), Converted (booked call), Parked (not now but future)
- Daily review: move prospects through stages, note responses, plan next actions

**Weekly metrics to track:**
- Connection requests sent
- Acceptance rate (target >40%)
- DM response rate (target >15%)
- Profile views from ICP
- Post engagement rates
- Discovery calls booked (the ultimate metric)

**network-reactivation.md — Warm network audit and reactivation plan:**

**Step 1: Audit existing connections (Day 1-2)**
- Go to LinkedIn Connections, use search filters to find ICP matches:
  - Search "Operating Partner" or "Portfolio" in connections
  - Search "CEO" or "CTO" or "Founder" with company size 11-200
  - Search by companies in PE, SaaS, funded startup space
- Create a shortlist of 50-100 warm connections matching ICP
- Categorize into PE / Startup / Enterprise buckets
- Note last interaction date and any shared history

**Step 2: Warm reactivation DM sequence (starts Day 1, parallel to everything else)**
- This is NOT cold outbound — these are people who already accepted a connection
- Reactivation DM 1: Casual reconnection. "Haven't connected in a while — I've been heads-down building AI systems for the past 5 months. Shipped 14 production deployments. Would love to hear what you're working on."
- Reactivation DM 2 (Day 3-5, if they respond): Share relevant insight based on their role/industry. "Saw you're at [company]. We just helped a similar [industry] company [specific result]. Happy to share what we learned."
- Reactivation DM 3 (Day 7, if conversation is warm): Offer free AI readiness assessment. "Would it be useful to do a quick 30-min AI readiness assessment for [company]? No charge — I'm looking to connect with [PE/startup] leaders who are thinking about AI."

Write 3 example reactivation DM sequences (one PE, one startup, one general) showing how to make it feel like genuine reconnection, not outreach.

**Volume:** 10-15 warm reactivation DMs per day in addition to cold outbound pipeline. Warm reactivation starts Day 1 (even before cold outbound).

**Why warm first:** Existing connections who respond have dramatically higher conversion rates than cold outbound because trust already exists. These are the fastest path to discovery calls.
