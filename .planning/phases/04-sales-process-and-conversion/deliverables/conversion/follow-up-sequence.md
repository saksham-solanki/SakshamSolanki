# 5-Email Follow-Up Sequence for Stalled Deals

**INTERNAL DOCUMENT -- Personalize every email before sending. Never send a template verbatim.**

---

## Usage Instructions

1. **Track all sends in HubSpot.** Deal stage should be "Negotiation" or "Proposal Sent." Log each email as an activity on the deal record so you have a complete history.

2. **Personalize EVERY email.** Replace all `[bracketed]` placeholders with specific data from the discovery call. The prospect should feel like this email was written for them, not pulled from a template.

3. **If the prospect responds at any point, STOP the sequence immediately.** Respond personally. A reply means they are engaged -- do not send the next templated email. Have a real conversation.

4. **After Email 5, set a 90-day follow-up reminder in HubSpot.** Add a note with the COI number and the reason they stalled. In 90 days, reach out with fresh context (a new case study, a relevant industry trend, or a direct question about their timeline).

5. **This sequence starts ONLY after a proposal has been delivered and the review meeting has occurred.** If the prospect goes silent before the review meeting, call them. Do not email.

6. **Spacing is intentional.** Do not compress the sequence. Day 3, 7, 10, 14, 21. Each gap gives them time to process without feeling pressured.

---

## Email 1: Case Study Sharing (Day 3)

**When:** 3 days after the proposal review meeting with no response.

**Purpose:** Deliver standalone value by sharing a directly relevant case study. This is not a follow-up -- it is useful content that would be worth reading even if they never buy.

---

**Subject line:** `[Relevant industry] case study -- similar to what we discussed`

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

After our conversation about [specific challenge discussed on the call], I thought this might be useful.

We worked with a [similar company description -- e.g., "90-person SaaS company" or "PE-backed logistics operation"] facing the same challenge: [one-sentence problem summary that mirrors the prospect's situation].

The result: **[primary metric -- e.g., "68% of support tickets now handled automatically, resolution time down 73%"]**.

The full case study is here: [link to case study on sakshamsolanki.com or attach a one-page PDF].

What stood out to me about your situation is [one specific parallel between the case study and their challenge -- e.g., "the volume of manual routing decisions your team handles weekly -- very similar to what this company was dealing with before the system went live"].

Happy to walk through how this would apply to [Company Name] specifically.

-- Saksham

---

**Case Study Selection Guide:**

| Prospect ICP | Prospect Challenge | Recommended Case Study | Lead Metric |
|-------------|-------------------|----------------------|-------------|
| PE firm | Operational inefficiency | Enterprise workflow automation | 120 hrs/week eliminated |
| PE firm | Portfolio-wide modernization | CRM pipeline automation | 3x meetings, $150/month cost |
| Startup | Support scaling | Support triage automation | 68% deflection |
| Startup | Feature velocity | AI wellness platform (Fitelo) | 50K users in 60 days |
| Startup | Content bottleneck | Content automation | 60 hrs/week saved, 8-day build |
| Enterprise | Manual workflows | Enterprise workflow automation | 120 hrs/week, 94% error reduction |
| Enterprise | Legacy integration | Fleet management or hotel system | 23% cost reduction or 28% RevPAR |
| Any | Sales pipeline | CRM pipeline automation | 3x qualified meetings |
| Any | Phone/voice operations | Voice agent deployment | 91% missed call reduction |

## Email 2: New Insight or Data Point (Day 7)

**When:** 7 days after the proposal review meeting (4 days after Email 1).

**Purpose:** Share a relevant industry insight that connects to their specific COI number. Position yourself as someone who thinks about their business even when they are not paying you.

---

**Subject line:** `Thought of [Company Name] when I saw this`

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Saw this [article / stat / research report / industry development] and it reminded me of our conversation:

[One paragraph summarizing the insight -- e.g., "A recent analysis found that companies in [their industry] lose an average of $X per year on manual [process type]. The companies that automated earliest captured 2-3x the savings because they avoided the hiring costs that come with scaling manual processes."]

This connects directly to the $[their annual COI] we calculated together. The [insight] suggests [specific implication for their business -- e.g., "the cost compounds faster than most teams expect, especially at your growth rate"].

Worth revisiting the timeline? The proposal is designed to deploy in [timeframe] -- we could have systems running before [specific date or milestone relevant to them -- e.g., "Q3 planning"].

-- Saksham

---

**Where to find insights:**
- Industry-specific newsletters and reports
- LinkedIn posts from thought leaders in their space
- New case studies from your own work (if relevant)
- Regulatory changes affecting their industry
- Competitor moves (if public information)

**Rule:** The insight must be genuinely useful. If the email would be worth reading even if they never buy from you, it is a good email. If it only exists to get a response, rewrite it.

## Email 3: Proactive Objection Addressing (Day 10)

**When:** 10 days after the proposal review meeting (3 days after Email 2).

**Purpose:** Address the most likely objection for their ICP before they raise it. This demonstrates that you understand their buying process and removes friction proactively.

---

**Subject line:** `The question most [their role -- e.g., "VP of Operations" / "CTOs" / "operating partners"] ask at this stage`

---

### ICP Variant: PE Firms

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

At this point in the conversation, most operating partners ask a version of the same question: "How does this scale across the portfolio?"

Fair question. Here is how it typically works:

The first deployment creates the playbook. We prove the model at [Portfolio Company Name], document the operational impact, and build the case study that makes every subsequent conversation easier. The operating partner uses that result as the template for the rest of the portfolio.

The EBITDA impact we projected for [Company Name] alone was $[annual savings]. Applied across [N] applicable portfolio companies, that becomes a $[total] value creation story -- the kind of number that strengthens the exit narrative.

If this resonates, let's get 15 minutes on the calendar to map out what a portfolio rollout would look like.

-- Saksham

---

### ICP Variant: Funded Startups

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

The question I hear most from CTOs at this stage: "Should we build this in-house instead?"

It is a good question. Here is how to think about it:

Hiring a senior AI engineer takes 6 months and costs $200K+/year. Before they ship anything production-ready, add another 6 months of ramp time. That is 12 months and $200K+ before a single system is live.

The Sprint deploys production systems in 60 days. Many of our clients eventually bring AI capability in-house -- but they do it after we deploy the first systems, because by then they know exactly what to build and can hire against a clear spec instead of a vague job description.

At your current burn rate, the difference between 60 days and 12 months is [X months of burn x monthly burn rate] = $[amount] in additional operational waste.

If this resonates, let's get 15 minutes on the calendar.

-- Saksham

---

### ICP Variant: Enterprise

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

The question that comes up most often from enterprise teams at this stage: "How do we get internal stakeholders aligned?"

Completely understandable. Here is what we have found works:

The proposal includes an executive summary specifically designed for internal circulation. It has the quantified business case, projected ROI, and risk assessment that procurement, IT, and finance need to see. We have built this document to answer the exact questions each stakeholder group typically asks.

If it would be helpful, I am available to join a brief call with your [CFO / CTO / IT director / procurement lead] to walk through the technical and financial details directly. This tends to accelerate the approval timeline by 2-3 weeks compared to circulating documents without context.

The $[annual COI] we calculated is accumulating at $[monthly COI]/month. If aligning stakeholders is the bottleneck, let me help remove it.

-- Saksham

## Email 4: Stakeholder Enablement (Day 14)

**When:** 14 days after the proposal review meeting (4 days after Email 3).

**Purpose:** Provide a ready-made asset they can forward to other decision-makers. Remove the friction of internal selling by giving them the ammunition.

---

**Subject line:** `One-page summary for your team`

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I put together a one-page executive summary of the [project name / engagement type] we discussed. It is designed to give your [CFO / CTO / board / procurement team] the key numbers without requiring them to read the full proposal.

[Attach the appropriate executive summary from Phase 1 -- Audit, Sprint, or Transformation -- customized with the prospect's specific COI number and projected outcomes.]

The document covers:
- The operational challenge and its annual cost ($[COI])
- The recommended solution and timeline
- Investment amount and projected payback period
- Two proof points from similar engagements

Feel free to forward it as-is or adapt it to your internal format.

If it would help, I am happy to join a brief call with your [relevant stakeholder title] to answer any questions directly. Building internal consensus is part of the process -- I have done this with [number of similar companies] companies and can help navigate it.

-- Saksham

---

**Note:** The executive summary attached should be the Phase 1 executive summary (Audit, Sprint, or Transformation) with placeholder values replaced using this prospect's specific data. Do not create a new document -- use the existing templates.

---

## Email 5: Graceful Close with Open Door (Day 21)

**When:** 21 days after the proposal review meeting (7 days after Email 4). This is the final email in the sequence.

**Purpose:** Acknowledge that timing may not be right without burning the bridge. Restate the COI one final time. Leave the door open for future engagement.

---

**Subject line:** `Closing the loop on [project name or engagement topic]`

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I wanted to close the loop on the [tier name] we discussed for [Company Name].

I understand timing may not be right. Decisions like this involve multiple stakeholders, competing priorities, and budget cycles -- that is the reality of running a business.

One number worth keeping in mind: the current process continues to cost approximately **$[monthly COI]/month** in labor and lost opportunities. That is **$[quarterly COI] per quarter** that accumulates whether a decision is made or not.

If the situation changes, I am here. No need to restart the conversation from scratch -- I have the diagnostic data, the COI calculations, and the proposal ready to update.

In the meantime, I will occasionally share relevant insights when I come across something applicable to [Company Name]'s situation.

-- Saksham

---

**After Email 5:**
- Set a 90-day follow-up reminder in HubSpot
- Add a deal note: "Sequence completed [date]. COI: $[amount]/year. Stall reason: [reason if known]. Revisit on [date]."
- Move deal stage to "Closed Lost" with reason "Timing" (not "No Decision" -- they may return)
- Add to quarterly check-in list

## Sequence Summary

| Email | Day | Subject Pattern | Core Value Delivered | CTA |
|-------|-----|----------------|---------------------|-----|
| 1 | 3 | Case study | Relevant proof point from similar company | Walk through how it applies |
| 2 | 7 | Industry insight | Useful stat or trend connected to their COI | Revisit the timeline |
| 3 | 10 | Proactive objection | Address most likely concern for their ICP | 15 minutes on the calendar |
| 4 | 14 | Stakeholder tool | Executive summary for internal circulation | Join a call with their stakeholders |
| 5 | 21 | Graceful close | Monthly COI reminder, open door | When timing is right, I'm here |

**What this sequence is NOT:**
- It is NOT a pressure campaign. Every email delivers value without demanding a response.
- It is NOT generic. Every email references specific numbers and context from the discovery call.
- It does NOT contain "just checking in," "following up on my last email," "wanted to circle back," "touching base," or any equivalent filler language. If the email does not deliver standalone value, do not send it.
