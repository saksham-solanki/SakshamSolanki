**INTERNAL DOCUMENT -- Never send this template directly. Always personalize with discovery call data.**

## Usage Instructions

**Read these before creating any proposal. Every rule exists because of a deal that was lost.**

1. **NEVER send a proposal without a review meeting booked.** This is the single most important rule. Say on the discovery call: "I'll send the proposal Tuesday. Let's book 30 minutes Thursday to walk through it together." If they decline the review meeting, do not send the proposal -- you do not have a real prospect.

2. **Deliver via Google Docs** (not PDF, not email attachment). Google Docs lets you see when they open it, how long they spend, and whether they share it with colleagues. Create from this template, share with edit access off (view only), and send the link.

3. **Send 24-48 hours before the review meeting** so they can read it first. This means the review meeting is a discussion, not a presentation. You spend the meeting answering questions and handling objections, not reading slides aloud.

4. **Adapt ALL placeholder markers** before sending. Every `[INSERT: ...]` marker must be replaced with specific data from the discovery call. A proposal with placeholder markers signals that you send the same document to everyone.

5. **Choose the correct ICP language variant** for Sections 1 and 6. PE firms, startups, and enterprise buyers use different vocabulary and care about different outcomes. The wrong language signals that you do not understand their world.

6. **Reference the COI number calculated together on the call.** The prospect watched you calculate this number in real time. Repeating it in the proposal anchors their decision against a figure they helped create -- not a number you invented.

7. **Proposal turnaround: 48 hours maximum.** Speed signals competence. If you take a week, they assume you are either unorganized or not prioritizing them.
