# Pre-Call Diagnostic Questionnaire

**When to send:** 24-48 hours before the scheduled discovery call
**Format:** Plain-text email (not a form -- feels more personal for high-ticket prospects)
**Purpose:** (1) Filter tire-kickers who won't complete it, (2) Prime the COI conversation by getting the prospect thinking in quantifiable terms before the call

---

## Email Template

**Subject:** Quick prep for our call on [Day]

Hi [Name],

To make the most of our time together, I'd like to understand your current situation before we speak. Please reply to this email with your answers to the seven questions below.

This should take about 10 minutes. The more specific you are, the more useful our conversation will be.

---

### Diagnostic Questions

1. **What is the specific operational challenge you're looking to solve?**
   Describe it in one paragraph -- the more specific, the better. What process is broken, slow, or expensive?

2. **How many people on your team are currently involved in this process?**
   Approximate number and their roles (e.g., "3 support agents + 1 manager reviewing tickets").

3. **Roughly how many hours per week does your team spend on this process?**
   Your best estimate across the entire team. Include time spent on workarounds, manual handoffs, and fixing errors.

4. **What tools or systems do you currently use for this workflow?**
   List everything involved -- CRM, helpdesk, spreadsheets, Slack channels, custom software, email. Include any tools that should be connected but aren't.

5. **Have you attempted to solve this problem before? What happened?**
   Previous vendors, internal builds, tools tried, or initiatives that stalled. What worked and what didn't?

6. **Who else will be involved in the decision to move forward?**
   Names and titles of key stakeholders who would need to approve an investment in solving this.

7. **What is your ideal timeline for having a solution in place?**
   When do you need this operational? Is there an external deadline driving the timing (board meeting, contract renewal, product launch)?

---

Looking forward to our conversation on [Day] at [Time].

-- Saksham

## Question-to-Discovery-Call Mapping

Each question is designed to feed a specific phase of the discovery call (see: `discovery-call-framework.md`).

| Question | Feeds Into | Purpose |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Q1: Operational challenge | Phase 2A -- Situation | Gives Saksham the starting point for the diagnostic; identifies the primary pain without spending call time on basics |
| Q2: Team size and roles | Phase 2B -- COI Question 1 | **COI-priming.** Gets the prospect thinking about how many people are tied up in this process. Directly feeds the labor cost calculation. |
| Q3: Hours per week | Phase 2B -- COI Question 1 | **COI-priming.** Combined with Q2, this gives Saksham the inputs to calculate labor cost before the call even starts. The prospect has already done the mental math. |
| Q4: Current tools | Phase 2A -- Situation | Maps the technical landscape; identifies integration requirements and disconnected systems that cause friction |
| Q5: Previous attempts | Phase 2A -- Situation + Phase 3 -- Prescription | Reveals what has been tried, what failed, and why. Prevents recommending something they already rejected. Also surfaces competitor exposure. |
| Q6: Decision stakeholders | Phase 2C -- BANT Authority | **BANT-priming.** Identifies the decision chain before the call. If they list 5 stakeholders, expect a longer sales cycle. If they list only themselves, Sprint-speed deal. |
| Q7: Timeline | Phase 2C -- BANT Timeline | **BANT-priming.** Reveals urgency. "Next quarter" = real deal. "Sometime next year" = nurture, not pursue. External deadlines create natural urgency. |

### COI Pre-Calculation

Before the call, use Q2 and Q3 to estimate a rough COI number:

```
Estimated Labor Cost = [Q2: team size] x [Q3: hours/week] x 52 x [estimated loaded hourly cost]
```

Use $50-$75/hour as the loaded cost estimate for US-based teams (adjust for market). This gives you a starting number to validate during the call -- you're not starting from zero.

## Usage Instructions for Saksham

### Sending

- Send via email reply (hit reply to the Calendly confirmation thread, or send a fresh email if no thread exists)
- Subject line: "Quick prep for our call on [Day]" -- casual, not corporate
- Send within 1 hour of the call being booked
- Do NOT use a Google Form, Typeform, or other tool. A plain email signals "this is a real conversation between two people," not a funnel step.

### Follow-Up If Incomplete

- **24 hours before the call, if no response:** Send one follow-up:

> "Hi [Name] -- just a quick reminder to send over your answers before our call tomorrow. Even brief responses will help me prepare. If you're short on time, the most important ones are Q1, Q2, and Q3."

- **At call time, if still incomplete:** Do NOT cancel. Use the first 10 minutes of the call to walk through the questions verbally. Adjust the diagnostic phase timing accordingly (compress Situation from 5 to 3 minutes, keep COI and BANT at full length).

### Pre-Call Review (3x3 Research Method)

When the questionnaire comes back, review it with this checklist:

**About the company (3 things):**
1. Recent news (funding, acquisitions, leadership changes, product launches)
2. Company size and growth trajectory (LinkedIn, Crunchbase)
3. Technology stack clues (job postings, case studies, their website)

**About the person (3 things):**
1. Their role and tenure (LinkedIn profile)
2. Content they've posted or engaged with (LinkedIn activity)
3. Mutual connections or shared context (conferences, communities)

**Annotate the questionnaire:** Write notes next to each answer. Flag vague answers that need probing. Pre-calculate the rough COI number from Q2 + Q3.

## Differentiation from Website Pre-Qualification Form

The `/solutions` page pre-qualification form (WEB-07) and this questionnaire serve different purposes at different funnel stages:

| Dimension | Website Pre-Qual Form | Pre-Call Questionnaire |
|-----------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Funnel stage** | Top of funnel -- first contact | Mid-funnel -- call already booked |
| **Fields** | 7 lightweight fields (name, email, company, role, challenge, budget range, timeline) | 7 deeper diagnostic questions requiring thought |
| **Effort required** | 2 minutes | 10 minutes |
| **Purpose** | Filter: is this person worth a call? | Prepare: make the call productive and prime COI |
| **Format** | Web form with dropdowns and short text | Plain-text email requiring written responses |
| **Data produced** | Qualification signal (should we book a call?) | Diagnostic data (what should we discuss on the call?) |

The website form decides IF a call happens. The questionnaire decides HOW the call goes.

*Send this questionnaire for every discovery call. The 10 minutes the prospect spends answering these questions saves 15 minutes of basic discovery on the call and produces a better COI calculation because they've already started thinking in numbers.*
