# ICP Messaging: Private Equity Firms

---

## 1. ICP Profile

### Persona A: Operating Partner (Champion / Referral Source)

- **Title:** Operating Partner, VP of Value Creation, Portfolio Operations Lead
- **Company:** Mid-market PE fund ($500M-$5B AUM), 10-40 portfolio companies
- **Budget:** Allocated from value creation fund or portfolio company P&L -- $15K-$1M+ depending on scope
- **Decision process:** Operating partner identifies the need, recommends to portfolio company CEO, CEO makes the buying decision. Operating partner is the champion who opens doors across the portfolio.
- **Evaluated on:** EBITDA growth, margin expansion, exit multiples, value creation plan execution

### Persona B: Portfolio Company CEO (Buyer / Decision Maker)

- **Title:** CEO, COO, or President of PE-backed company
- **Company:** $10M-$500M revenue, 50-2000 employees, under board mandate to modernize
- **Budget:** Operating budget with board oversight -- needs to justify ROI to the investment committee
- **Decision process:** Receives recommendation from operating partner or identifies the problem independently. Needs a clear business case with projected P&L impact before committing.
- **Evaluated on:** Revenue growth, cost reduction, headcount efficiency, customer retention, operational risk management

## 2. Pain Points (Ranked by Urgency)

### Pain Point 1: AI Pilots That Die in the Lab

**Statement:** 90% of AI pilots never advance beyond experimental stages.

**Data:** Accenture mid-market PE research confirms that portfolio companies try AI, build a demo, then it sits unused because nobody planned for production deployment, change management, or integration with existing operations.

**What they say:** "We did a proof of concept last year. The team loved it during the demo. Then nothing happened."

**What they mean:** "We spent $200K on consultants who gave us a strategy deck and a prototype that nobody uses. I need someone who actually deploys production systems."

---

### Pain Point 2: No Measurable KPIs for AI Investments

**Statement:** Only 36% of PE firms with AI strategies have specific KPIs for measuring AI impact.

**Data:** FTI Consulting found that the vast majority of AI initiatives in PE portfolio companies have no defined success metrics -- making it impossible to prove ROI to the investment committee.

**What they say:** "The board keeps asking what our AI strategy is delivering. I don't have a good answer."

**What they mean:** "We cannot attribute a single dollar of EBITDA impact to anything we have done with AI. I need someone who defines measurable targets before they start building."

---

### Pain Point 3: Portfolio Companies Still Running on Spreadsheets

**Statement:** 42% of mid-market portfolio companies report very low adoption of key digital technologies.

**Data:** Accenture found that nearly half of mid-market companies have not moved beyond basic tools -- still running core operations on spreadsheets, email chains, and WhatsApp groups.

**What they say:** "Our portfolio companies know they need to modernize, but they don't know where to start."

**What they mean:** "These companies need someone who can walk in, identify the highest-value opportunities, and deploy systems that show results -- without requiring a 12-month transformation initiative."

---

### Pain Point 4: Strategy on Paper, Not in Operations

**Statement:** Less than 15% of portfolio companies track EBIT or revenue impact of AI initiatives, despite 60% claiming to have AI strategies.

**Data:** Accenture research shows that the gap between "having a strategy" and "executing against measurable targets" is where most portfolio companies lose credibility with the investment committee.

**What they say:** "We have an AI strategy. It's in the board deck."

**What they mean:** "Nothing has been deployed. Nothing is being measured. The strategy exists in a document, not in our operations."

---

### Pain Point 5: Workforce Not Ready for Change

**Statement:** Two-thirds of employees say AI advances faster than workforce training.

**Data:** Accenture found that companies deploying AI without corresponding training and change management see "organ rejection" -- teams resist the new systems, revert to old processes, and the investment is wasted.

**What they say:** "We deployed a tool, but the team won't use it."

**What they mean:** "Nobody trained the team on how this changes their daily work. They see it as a threat, not a tool. We need someone who handles the human side, not just the technology."

## 3. Messaging Pillars

### Pillar 1: Production Systems, Not Prototypes

"90% of AI pilots die in the lab. We deploy production systems that show up on the P&L."

Every engagement ends with deployed, working infrastructure that your team uses daily -- not a strategy deck that collects dust. We define the target metric before we build and measure against it after deployment.

### Pillar 2: EBITDA Impact You Can Report to the Committee

"Every system we deploy comes with a quantified business case and measurable outcomes."

We build the ROI analysis into the engagement from day one. Operating partners get a clear narrative for the investment committee. Portfolio company CEOs get metrics they can track monthly.

### Pillar 3: Speed That Matches Your Hold Period

"Deployed in 45-60 days, not 12-18 months. Value creation happens during the hold period, not after it."

Your hold period is finite. Every month spent in planning, hiring, or "exploring" is a month of value creation lost. We compress the timeline from diagnosis to deployed systems because the team has done this across 500+ projects.
