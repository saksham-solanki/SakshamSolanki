# Value-Based Pricing Framework

**INTERNAL DOCUMENT -- Never share with clients.**

This is your personal reference for pricing discovery calls. Everything here is designed to help you calculate, justify, and present pricing confidently.

---

## 1. Pricing Philosophy

**Core principle:** Price at 10-25% of projected Year 1 business impact.

You are not selling time. You are not selling features. You are selling a measurable reduction in operational cost or increase in revenue. The price is a fraction of the value delivered.

**Rules:**
- NEVER quote an hourly rate. Not even when asked. Respond with: "We price based on business impact, not time."
- NEVER quote a day rate. Same reason.
- NEVER use cost-plus pricing ("it takes X hours at $Y rate"). That anchors to cost, not value.
- ALWAYS calculate Cost of Inaction FIRST. Once the client hears the problem costs them $300K/year, a $85K investment feels small.
- ALWAYS use conservative projections. If reality exceeds projections, the client sees you as someone who under-promises and over-delivers. That generates referrals.

## 2. Recommended Price Ranges Per Tier

### AI Opportunity Audit: $15,000 - $40,000

**Pricing type:** Fixed fee (not value-based -- this is the entry point relationship builder).

**Justification:** The workshop format is significantly more hands-on than typical diagnostics. You work directly with the buyer's team in a half-day or full-day session, not from a survey. The deliverable is a polished AI Opportunity Report with quantified opportunities and an implementation roadmap. This sits at the top of the audit market.

**Starting recommendation for first 2-3 deals:** $15,000-$20,000. Increase as case studies at this tier accumulate. Goal: move to $25,000-$35,000 within 6 months.

**Payment:** 100% upfront before the workshop.

### AI Operations Sprint: $75,000 - $200,000

**Pricing type:** Value-based at 10-15% of Year 1 projected impact.

**Justification:** This is a multi-system deployment in 45-60 days -- not a single chatbot. The Sprint bundles automation, integration, and team training into one outcome-driven engagement. Market rate for comparable mid-complexity implementations runs $40K-$150K. You are at the top of boutique range, justified by execution speed and integration depth.

**Starting recommendation for first 2-3 deals:** $75,000-$100,000. Increase as measurable results from deployed Sprints create proof points for higher pricing.

**Payment:** 50% upfront at agreement, 50% at deployment.

### AI Transformation Program: $250,000 - $1,000,000+

**Pricing type:** Value-based at 15-25% of Year 1 projected impact.

**Justification:** Full-stack operational overhaul spanning 3-6 months. Multiple systems, executive dashboards, team training, change management. Comparable to mid-market enterprise AI engagements. PE-backed companies expect investment at this level for comprehensive modernization.

**Starting recommendation for first 2-3 deals:** $250,000-$350,000. Scale with engagement complexity and demonstrated portfolio-level impact.

**Payment:** Milestone-based. Typical structure: 30% at kickoff, 30% at architecture approval, 30% at deployment, 10% at optimization completion.

**Note:** Start at the lower end of recommended ranges for the first 2-3 deals, then increase as case studies accumulate at each price tier. Your confidence will grow with each successful engagement. Let the results justify the price increases.

## 3. Cost of Inaction (COI) Framework

This is the single most important pricing tool. 40-60% of enterprise deals are lost not to competitors but to "do nothing." The COI framework makes doing nothing feel more expensive than hiring you.

**The 5-Question Discovery Framework:**

Use these questions live on a discovery call. They produce a dollar number in real time that anchors the entire pricing conversation.

**Question 1:** "How many hours does your team spend on [manual process] per week?"
- Listen for the number. If they hedge ("probably 30-40 hours"), use the conservative end.
- This gets them thinking about the problem in concrete terms.

**Question 2:** "What's the fully-loaded cost of those hours?"
- Fully-loaded = salary + benefits + overhead. Rule of thumb: 1.3-1.5x base salary.
- If they don't know: "What's the average salary of the people doing this work?" Then multiply by 1.4 and divide by 2,080 (work hours per year) for the hourly loaded cost.

**Question 3:** "How many leads, customers, or opportunities do you lose because of this?"
- This is the revenue side. Slow response times cause churn. Manual errors lose deals. Bottlenecks delay revenue.
- If they can't quantify: "What percentage of your tickets, leads, or orders are affected by delays?"

**Question 4:** "What's the revenue impact of that loss?"
- Multiply the volume from Q3 by their average customer value or deal size.
- Even a conservative estimate makes the number real.

**Question 5:** "How much worse does this get in 12 months without action?"
- Growth multiplier. If they are growing 20% year-over-year, the problem grows 20% too.
- This creates urgency: the problem is not static, it compounds.

**Total COI = (Labor Cost + Revenue Loss) x Growth Multiplier**

The power is in the conversation, not the spreadsheet. Keep it simple enough to run live on a call. Write the numbers on a shared screen or send them in the chat. The client should see the COI number calculated in front of them.

## 4. ROI Calculator

### Inputs (Gathered During Discovery Call)

| Input | How to Get It |
|-------|---------------|
| Number of employees on the manual task | "How many people are involved in this process?" |
| Hours per week spent on the task | "How many hours per week total across the team?" |
| Fully-loaded cost per hour | Annual salary / 2,080 hours x 1.4 overhead factor |
| Revenue lost due to current problem | Churn, missed leads, errors, delays -- from Q3 and Q4 above |
| Annual growth rate | "What's your growth rate?" (use for compounding the problem) |

### Outputs

| Output | Formula |
|--------|---------|
| Annual Labor Cost of Current State | Employees x Hours/week x 52 x Loaded hourly cost |
| Annual Revenue Loss | Lost customers/leads x Average value |
| Total Annual Cost of Inaction | Labor Cost + Revenue Loss |
| Year 2 COI (with growth) | Total COI x (1 + Growth Rate) |
| Conservative Savings (40% reduction) | Total COI x 0.40 |
| Moderate Savings (60% reduction) | Total COI x 0.60 |
| Aggressive Savings (80% reduction) | Total COI x 0.80 |
| Recommended Investment | 10-25% of Conservative Year 1 Savings |
| Payback Period | Investment / (Monthly Savings) |
| 3-Year Net Value | (Conservative Annual Savings x 3) - Investment |

### Worked Example: Support Triage Automation

**Scenario:** A 90-person SaaS company with a manual support triage process.

**Inputs gathered on discovery call:**
- 5 support agents spending combined 40 hours/week on manual triage
- Loaded cost: $50/hour ($70K salary x 1.4 / 2,080)
- 15% of tickets churn because response time is too slow
- 400 tickets/week, average customer value $200/year
- Company growing 25% year-over-year

**Calculations:**

| Line Item | Calculation | Amount |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Annual labor cost | 40 hrs x 52 weeks x $50/hr | $104,000/year |
| Annual revenue loss from churn | 400 tickets x 15% x $200 ACV x 52/400 | ~$31,200/year |
| **Total Annual COI** | $104,000 + $31,200 | **$135,200/year** |
| Year 2 COI (25% growth) | $135,200 x 1.25 | $169,000/year |
| Conservative savings (40%) | $135,200 x 0.40 | $54,080/year |
| Moderate savings (60%) | $135,200 x 0.60 | $81,120/year |
| Aggressive savings (80%) | $135,200 x 0.80 | $108,160/year |

**Pricing the Sprint:**
- At 15% of conservative savings: $54,080 x 0.15 = ~$8K (too low for a multi-system bundle)
- Multi-system Sprint justifies $75,000-$100,000 because it includes triage automation, CRM integration, escalation routing, and analytics dashboard
- At moderate savings ($81K/year), a $85K Sprint pays for itself in Year 1 and saves $81K+ every year after
- **3-Year Net Value:** ($81,120 x 3) - $85,000 = **$158,360**

**How to present this:** "Based on the $135K annual cost we just calculated together, the investment for an AI Operations Sprint is $85K. That means you break even in Year 1 and save over $80K every year after. Over three years, that's $158K in net savings."

## 5. Pricing Conversation Playbook

### When to Discuss Pricing

ONLY after the COI has been calculated and value has been established. Never lead with price. Never answer "how much does this cost?" before the discovery call. If asked over email: "Pricing depends on the specific opportunity and projected impact. Let's get on a call to scope it properly."

### How to Present

After calculating COI together on the call:

"Based on the $[COI amount] annual cost we just calculated, the investment for [tier name] is $[price]. That means you [break even / see positive ROI] in [timeframe] and save $[amount] every year after."

Key: use the word "investment," not "cost" or "price." The client just heard their COI number -- your fee is framed against that, not in isolation.

### Handling Objections

**"That's expensive."**
"I understand. The question is not whether $85K is a lot -- it's whether $135K per year in ongoing operational costs is acceptable. Every quarter you wait is another $34K."

**"Can you do it for less?"**
"We can reduce scope. If we focus on just the triage automation without the CRM integration, we could bring it to $[lower number]. But that leaves $[amount] in savings on the table. Most clients prefer to capture the full opportunity."

**"We need to think about it."**
"Of course. While you're deciding, the current process continues to cost $[monthly COI] per month. I'll send over a summary of what we discussed so your team has the numbers."

**"We're comparing other vendors."**
"Great -- you should. When you compare, ask each vendor: what specific operational outcome are you guaranteeing, and what's the timeline to deployed, production-ready systems? That will separate the strategy firms from the execution partners."

### Internal Pricing Check

If the client says yes immediately without any pushback: you probably priced too low. Note it for next time.

If the client ghosts after hearing the price: the COI was not established strongly enough, or they lack budget authority. The price is not the problem -- the value case was not made.
