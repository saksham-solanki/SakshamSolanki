- Qualification: Companies with identified automation opportunities (ideally from an Audit), $10M+ revenue, existing tech stack to integrate with

Create the pricing framework document. This is an INTERNAL document (never shared with clients) that Saksham uses to calculate and justify pricing during discovery calls.

**Document structure:**

**Section 1: Pricing Philosophy**
- Value-based pricing at 10-25% of projected Year 1 business impact
- NEVER hourly rates. NEVER "day rates." NEVER cost-plus.
- Price anchoring: Always calculate Cost of Inaction FIRST, then position investment as a fraction of the problem cost
- Conservative bias: Always use conservative projections. If reality exceeds projections, client sees under-promise/over-deliver.

**Section 2: Recommended Price Ranges Per Tier**
- AI Opportunity Audit: $15,000 - $40,000 (fixed fee, not value-based -- this is the entry point)
  - Justification: Workshop is more hands-on than typical diagnostics. Includes live session with buyer's team. At top of audit market.
  - Starting recommendation for first 2-3 deals: $15,000-$20,000
- AI Operations Sprint: $75,000 - $200,000
  - Value capture rate: 10-15% of Year 1 projected impact
  - Justification: Multi-system deployment in 45-60 days. Market rate for medium AI implementations.
  - Starting recommendation: $75,000-$100,000
- AI Transformation Program: $250,000 - $1,000,000+
  - Value capture rate: 15-25% of Year 1 projected impact
  - Justification: Full-stack overhaul, 3-6 months. Comparable to mid-market enterprise engagements.
  - Starting recommendation: $250,000-$350,000
- Note: "Start at the lower end of recommended ranges for first 2-3 deals, then increase as case studies accumulate at the new price tier" (per STATE.md blocker)

**Section 3: Cost of Inaction (COI) Framework**
- The 5-question discovery framework that produces a dollar number on a call:
  1. "How many hours does your team spend on [manual process] per week?"
  2. "What's the fully-loaded cost of those hours?" (salary + benefits + overhead, typically 1.3-1.5x base)
  3. "How many leads/customers/opportunities do you lose because of this?"
  4. "What's the revenue impact of that loss?"
  5. "How much worse does this get in 12 months without action?" (growth multiplier)
- Total COI = (Labor cost + Revenue loss) x Growth multiplier
- The power is in the conversation, not the spreadsheet. Keep it simple enough to run live on a call.

**Section 4: ROI Calculator**
- Inputs (gathered during discovery call):
  - Number of employees doing the manual task
  - Hours per week spent on the task
  - Fully-loaded hourly cost (or annual salary / 2080 hours)
  - Revenue lost due to current problem (churn, missed leads, errors)
  - Annual growth rate (how much worse without action)
- Outputs:
  - Annual Cost of Current State
  - Annual Cost of Inaction (Year 1 with growth)
  - Projected Savings: Conservative (40% reduction), Moderate (60%), Aggressive (80%)
  - Recommended Investment: 10-25% of Conservative Year 1 savings
  - Payback Period in months
  - 3-Year Net Value (savings minus investment)
- Include a worked example using the support triage scenario from research:
  - 40 hrs/week manual triage, $50/hr loaded cost = $104K/year labor
  - 15% churn from slow response, 400 tickets/week x $200 ACV = $31K/year revenue loss
  - Total COI: $135K/year
  - Conservative savings (40%): $54K/year
  - Sprint price at 15%: ~$20K (but multi-system bundle justifies $75K-$100K)
  - Moderate savings (60%): $81K/year -- Sprint pays for itself in Year 1

**Section 5: Pricing Conversation Playbook**
- When to discuss pricing: ONLY after COI has been calculated and value established
- How to present: "Based on the $135K annual cost we just calculated, the investment for an AI Operations Sprint is $85K. That means you break even in Year 1 and save $85K+ every year after."
- Handling "that's expensive": Reframe against COI, not against competitors. "The question isn't whether $85K is a lot -- it's whether $135K per year in ongoing costs is acceptable."
- If client says yes immediately without pushback: "You probably priced too low" (internal note)
- Payment terms: 50% upfront, 50% at delivery (Sprint/Transformation). 100% upfront (Audit).

**Section 6: Competitive Pricing Context**
- Big 4 charges $150K-$1M+ for strategy ONLY (no deployed systems)
- Boutique AI firms charge $75K-$500K for strategy + some implementation
- AI automation agencies charge $2K-$15K for single chatbots
- Saksham's position: Top of boutique range, justified by full-stack execution capability
- Key message: "We charge less than McKinsey and deliver 10x more -- deployed production systems, not a PDF"

**Voice:** Internal, practical, conversational. This document is Saksham's personal reference for pricing calls. Write as if coaching him through a pricing conversation.
