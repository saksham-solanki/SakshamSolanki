5. Two-thirds of employees say AI advances faster than workforce training (Accenture)

Case study mapping (from research):
- CRM Pipeline Automation: $150/mo, 3x meetings, no AI -- THIS IS THE STAR CASE STUDY for PE. It proves Saksham solves business problems, not technology problems.

- Support triage cases: 68% deflection, 73% faster resolution
- Voice agents: 200+ calls/day, 91% missed call reduction

Lead message: "90% of AI pilots die in the lab. We deploy production systems that show up on the P&L."
Exit narrative angle: "AI capabilities built during our engagement become part of the exit narrative."
Recommended entry: Audit for operating partners (they champion across portfolio), Sprint for portfolio company CEOs who already know their problem

**Part B: Create the Viithiisys team narrative (11-team-narrative.md)**

This is an internal document -- a script/reference for how Saksham talks about his team on discovery calls and in proposals.

**Structure:**

1. **THE ONE-LINER** (use when briefly asked "who does the work?"):
   "I personally lead strategy and architecture. Execution is handled by my dedicated engineering team -- senior developers I've worked with across 500+ projects."

2. **THE FULL NARRATIVE** (use when client wants more detail, ~30 seconds spoken):
   Based on research recommendation but adapted to sound natural when spoken aloud. Key elements:
   - "I" lead strategy -- personal accountability
   - "Dedicated engineering team" -- not freelancers, not contractors
   - "500+ projects" -- credibility through track record, not headcount
   - Speed as proof: "45-60 days only possible because the team is experienced and cohesive"
   - Never say "outsourced" or "offshore" -- say "dedicated" and "senior"

3. **OBJECTION HANDLING** for team questions:
   - "How big is your team?" -- "The core team is [X] senior engineers. For larger engagements, I scale the team to match the scope. Every project gets my direct oversight on strategy and architecture."
   - "Are they full-time?" -- "They're dedicated to my engagements, yes. This isn't a freelancer marketplace -- these are engineers I've worked with for years."
   - "Where are they based?" -- "The team works across time zones, which means we often deliver faster because work continues around the clock. But I'm your single point of contact for everything."
   - "Can we meet the team?" -- "Absolutely. On larger engagements, you'll work directly with the lead engineer on your project. I'll introduce them during the architecture phase."

4. **WHAT TO NEVER SAY:**
   - Never mention "Viithiisys" by name
   - Never say "outsourced" or "offshore"
   - Never say "contractor" or "freelancer"
   - Never apologize for the model -- position it as an advantage (speed, cost efficiency, experienced team)
   - Never compare to agencies ("we're not an agency")

5. **WHY THIS MODEL IS AN ADVANTAGE** (talking points):
   - Client gets senior strategic attention (not handed off to junior analysts like Big 4)
   - Dedicated team means no ramp-up time (unlike hiring internally)
   - 500+ projects of experience means fewer mistakes, faster delivery
   - Cost advantage passed to client (no Big 4 overhead, no office in Manhattan)

**Voice:** Conversational, as if coaching Saksham. Write things he would actually say on a call, not corporate copy.
